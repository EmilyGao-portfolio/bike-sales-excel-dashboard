# Bike Purchase Customer Segmentation Analysis
## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer demographic data to identify the characteristics most associated with bike purchases. An interactive Excel dashboard was developed using PivotTables, PivotCharts, slicers, and data cleaning techniques to support customer segmentation and marketing decision-making.
## Business Questions
1. Which customer demographics are most likely to purchase a bike?
2. Which customer characteristics should marketing teams prioritize when targeting potential bike buyers?
## Tools
- Microsoft Excel
- PivotTables
- PivotCharts
- Slicers
- Data cleaning

## Key Insights
- Middle-aged customers represent the largest group of bike purchasers, suggesting this segment has the strongest purchase potential.
(This is clearly supported by your Customer Age Brackets chart.)
- Customers with shorter commute distances (0–1 mile) purchased bikes more frequently than those with longer commutes, indicating commute distance may influence purchasing behavior.
(Supported by the Customer Commute visualization.)
-Female customers who purchased bikes had a higher average income than female non-purchasers, while male income showed a smaller difference between purchasers and non-purchasers.
(Supported by your Average Income Per Purchase chart.)

## Business Recommendations
1. Prioritize marketing campaigns toward middle-aged customer segments, where bike purchase activity appears strongest.
2. Use demographic filters such as commute distance, education, and region to create more targeted marketing campaigns for high-potential customer groups.
